[EMBEDDED VIDEO: The Birth of Hip-Hop — DJ Kool Herc]

Transcript available below · Closed captions enabled

This documentary explores the origins of hip-hop in the South Bronx during the 1970s. We trace the movement from DJ Kool Herc's legendary block parties at 1520 Sedgwick Avenue — widely considered the birthplace of hip-hop — through the innovations of Grandmaster Flash, the emergence of MCing, b-boying, and graffiti art as a unified cultural expression. The film features interviews with pioneers and archival footage spanning 1973 through the Golden Age of the late 1980s.

Chapter 1 (0:00–8:40): The Bronx in the early 1970s — social context, community, and the first block parties.
Chapter 2 (8:41–18:20): DJ Kool Herc's technique — the "Merry-Go-Round" and the birth of the breakbeat.
Chapter 3 (18:21–28:55): Grandmaster Flash and the science of DJing.
Chapter 4 (28:56–38:10): Afrika Bambaataa and the Universal Zulu Nation.
Chapter 5 (38:11–45:00): The four elements and hip-hop's global spread.
